## History Module — "Roots of the Practice"

A standalone, deeply-researched section presented as an interactive timeline.

### Unit 1: Ancient Origins
- Hindu Upanishads (Mandukya, ~800–500 BCE) — the four states of consciousness, including *svapna* (dream)
- Early references in the Rigveda to dream awareness
- Greek and Roman dream traditions (Aristotle's *On Dreams*, Galen)

### Unit 2: Tibetan Dream Yoga
- Origins in Indian Buddhist tantra, transmitted to Tibet (~8th–11th century)
- Naropa's Six Yogas — *milam* (dream yoga) as one of the six
- The Bön tradition's parallel teachings (Tenzin Wangyal Rinpoche's lineage)
- Core aim: recognizing dreams as a path to recognizing the illusory nature of waking reality and preparing for the bardo

### Unit 3: Other Contemplative Traditions
- Sufi *khayal* and dream practices
- Indigenous dreamwork (Senoi, Aboriginal Australian, Iroquois)
- Daoist sleep practices

### Unit 4: Western Rediscovery
- Marquis d'Hervey de Saint-Denys (1867) — first systematic study
- Frederik van Eeden coining "lucid dream" (1913)
- Freud and Jung's dream frameworks

### Unit 5: Modern Scientific Era
- Stephen LaBerge's Stanford research (1980s) — first lab-verified lucid dreams via eye signals
- Paul Tholey's reflection technique
- Contemporary neuroscience: prefrontal cortex activation, gamma waves
- Therapeutic applications (nightmare treatment, PTSD)

### Unit 6: Convergence
- How traditional and scientific approaches inform each other today

## Course Curriculum (8–12 weeks)

Structured as progressive levels:

- **Foundation** — dream recall, journaling, sleep hygiene
- **Awareness** — reality checks, mindfulness throughout the day
- **Induction** — MILD, WILD, WBTB techniques alongside traditional Tibetan methods (visualization of the throat chakra, Guru Yoga before sleep)
- **Stabilization** — staying lucid, dream control vs. surrender
- **Deepening** — dream yoga proper: recognizing emptiness, working with fear, practicing virtues in the dream state
- **Integration** — bardo teachings, sleep yoga (awareness in dreamless sleep), waking-life application

Each lesson combines video, text, audio guidance, and a journaling prompt.

## Community (Self-Sustaining)

Designed to run with minimal staff oversight — peer-driven rather than teacher-driven.

- **Discussion forums** organized by topic and by experience level
- **Dream sharing circles** — small opt-in groups (8–12 people), asynchronous, witnessing without interpretation unless invited
- **Practice partners** matching system for accountability
- **Regional meetups** — discoverable through the platform, organized by members themselves
- **Clear code of conduct** rooted in respect for personal experience, cultural traditions, and the vulnerability of sharing dream content
- **Volunteer moderators** drawn from advanced students once the community matures

Light-touch moderation. No live events to staff.

## Support the Project

Everything is free. Donation model in the spirit of *dāna* (generosity).

- **Buy me a coffee** — one-time tips of any size, no account required
- **Buy someone a coffee** — once sustainable, tips roll forward symbolically (access is already open to everyone)
- Transparent running total of contributions and how funds are used
- No paywalls, no tiers, no premium content. Ever.

The dream-yoga traditions have always been transmitted through generosity, not commerce.

## Tone & Design Notes

- Treat the contemplative history with genuine respect — not exoticized
- Visual language: calm and spacious, deep blues / warm golds OR refined editorial black-on-white
- Generous whitespace, contemplative typography
- No productivity-app or mystical-clickbait aesthetics
- The free/donation model felt throughout — no upsell prompts, no "unlock" language, no scarcity tactics
