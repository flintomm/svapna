# Category structure

Twelve top-level categories. The first six mirror the site sections so the forum and the course feel like one place. The next four are the community spaces the OUTLINE.md describes. The last two are administrative.

---

## I. Foundations

**Tagline:** Practice, journaling, sleep, and the first months of the work.

**Description:**
For the early phases of the path — dream recall, journaling, sleep hygiene, reality checks, mindfulness in the day. Most members spend their first season here. Post about what's working, what isn't, what your dream journal looks like, and the small practical questions ("how do I remember more dreams?") that nobody else seems to ask out loud. Beginners are not a separate room. Everyone began here.

---

## II. History

**Tagline:** Roots of the practice — Mandukya to LaBerge.

**Description:**
The deeply-researched part of Svapna, organized as six units: ancient origins (the Mandukya Upanishad, Aristotle's *On Dreams*), Tibetan dream yoga (*milam* in Naropa's Six Yogas; the Bön lineage), other contemplative traditions (Sufi *khayal*, Senoi, Iroquois, Daoist), the Western rediscovery (Hervey de Saint-Denys, van Eeden, Freud, Jung), the modern scientific era (LaBerge, Tholey, contemporary neuroscience), and convergence. Discussion, primary sources, corrections welcome.

---

## III. Curriculum

**Tagline:** The twelve-week course, week by week.

**Description:**
Each week of the course has a thread. Bring questions about the lessons, share what came up in practice, post your journal extracts if you'd like a witness. The course runs in six phases — *Foundation, Awareness, Induction, Stabilization, Deepening, Integration* — and you can move at any pace. There's no cohort and no clock.

---

## IV. Community

**Tagline:** Introductions, gathering points, regional notes.

**Description:**
Where new members say hello and where regional meetups self-organize. If you're in Berlin, Mexico City, or rural Vermont and you'd like to find others nearby, post here. We don't run events centrally — members organize them themselves, and the platform is here to help you find each other.

---

## V. Library

**Tagline:** Texts, papers, films, glossary.

**Description:**
A reading list curated and continually expanded by members. Primary historical sources, contemporary studies, recommended films, the Sanskrit/Tibetan glossary. Submit additions, request things, post recovered translations, share annotated bibliographies. The Library is meant to outlive any single contributor.

---

## VI. Support

**Tagline:** A footnote, in the spirit of *dāna*.

**Description:**
A quiet category. If the work here has helped you and you're in a position to give, *Buy me a coffee* takes one-time tips of any amount, no account required; *Buy someone a coffee* lets the gesture roll forward symbolically. A transparent running total of contributions and how funds are used is pinned at the top. Otherwise the category is fine to ignore.

---

## VII. Dream sharing circles

**Tagline:** Small opt-in groups, asynchronous, witnessing without interpretation.

**Description:**
Dream sharing in groups of 8–12 people. Asynchronous — no live calls. We witness without interpretation unless you invite it. The norm here, drawn from the Senoi and several other traditions on the site, is that a dream belongs to the dreamer; nobody tells you what your dream meant. To request matching into a circle, post in the *Practice partners* category.

---

## VIII. Practice partners

**Tagline:** Accountability matching for the work.

**Description:**
For finding a practice partner — someone you check in with about reality checks, dream recall, MILD/WBTB attempts, and the long quiet stretches when nothing seems to be happening. Post what you're working on and what you'd like in a partner. We'll keep the matching as light-touch as we can.

---

## IX. Experiences

**Tagline:** Lucid moments, breakthroughs, plateaus, fear, returns.

**Description:**
What it's actually like, in practice. Lucid moments — the small ones especially. Breakthroughs and the long flat stretches between them. Working with fear in dreams. Stabilization techniques that worked or didn't. The aftermath of a strong dream into waking life. Post here without preamble; we'll meet you where you are.

---

## X. Lineage & teachers

**Tagline:** Acknowledgments, recommended teachers, ethical caveats.

**Description:**
The course draws on traditions that have living teachers and active lineages. This category is for naming them properly, for recommending books and retreat centers, and for raising ethical caveats when they belong. We treat the source traditions with genuine respect — that's not a slogan, it's a category-level commitment.

---

## XI. Meta

**Tagline:** Code of conduct, moderation, site feedback, corrections.

**Description:**
How this place runs. The full code of conduct lives here, pinned. Moderation logs, feedback on the site, requests for new categories, corrections to course material — all in this category. Volunteer moderators are drawn from longtime members; the path to becoming one runs through this category.

---

## XII. Off-topic

**Tagline:** The teahouse.

**Description:**
For everything else: book recommendations not tied to the course, conversations about contemplative life more broadly, the occasional question about a film. Light moderation. Stay kind.
