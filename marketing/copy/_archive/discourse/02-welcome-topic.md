# Welcome to Svapna

*Awaken within the dream.*

This is a course in dream yoga — the contemplative art of becoming aware within the dream state, and the lineage of teachers who passed it down. You're welcome here whether you've been practicing lucid dreaming for years, you've been sitting with a Tibetan teacher, or you wrote down a strange dream this morning and started looking.

---

## Where to begin

If you're new — open the **Curriculum** and start with *Foundation*. The first lessons are about dream recall and journaling. They're plain, useful, and they work. Begin tonight.

If you'd rather wander first — the **History** module is the deeply-researched part of the site, six units from the Mandukya Upanishad through Stephen LaBerge's Stanford lab. It's evergreen and asynchronous; read at any pace.

If you've been practicing for a while — the **Deepening** and **Integration** phases are where dream yoga proper begins: emptiness, fear, virtues practiced in the dream state, the bardo teachings, sleep yoga.

---

## How this place works

- **Forums** are organized by topic and by experience level. Post freely.
- **Dream sharing circles** are small opt-in groups of 8–12, asynchronous. We witness without interpretation unless you invite it. If you'd like to be matched, look for the *Practice partners* category.
- **Code of conduct**: respect for personal experience, respect for the traditions, and care with the vulnerability of sharing dream content. The full version is pinned in *Meta*.
- **Moderation** is light-touch and peer-driven. Volunteer moderators are drawn from longtime members.
- **No live events.** Everything here runs asynchronously, so you can practice on your own clock.

---

## A note on translation

Across the course you'll see Sanskrit, Tibetan, and a few other languages in their original spelling, with translations alongside. We've tried to treat the source traditions with genuine respect rather than aestheticizing them. If you spot something that reads as exoticization, please tell us in the *Meta* category — that's exactly the kind of correction we want.
